Foundational Textbooks

17th Century Context

The birth of modern calculus with Newton's method of fluxions and Leibniz's differential calculus. This century saw the first systematic approaches to handling infinitesimals and rates of change.

18th Century Context

The Age of Enlightenment brought systematic exposition and teaching of calculus. Euler's contributions to notation and Agnesi's pioneering work made calculus more accessible to students.

19th Century Context

The era of rigorization. Mathematicians like Cauchy, Weierstrass, and Riemann established the epsilon-delta foundations that form the basis of modern analysis.

20th Century Context

The century of pedagogical innovation and synthesis. Textbooks began balancing rigorous theory with practical applications, making advanced mathematics accessible to broader audiences.

21st Century Context

The digital age of mathematics education. Interactive textbooks, visualization tools, and open-access resources have transformed how calculus and analysis are taught and learned.
